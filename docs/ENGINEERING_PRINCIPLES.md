# ENGINEERING_PRINCIPLES.md

> This document defines the engineering principles that serve as the foundation for the development of Lunar Core.

---

## 1. Write with Purpose

Every part of the system—including classes, functions, blocks, hooks, and dependencies—should have a clear purpose.

Features that provide no meaningful value should not be added.

---

## 2. Architecture First

A well-designed architecture is the foundation of long-term development.

Decisions about the system structure should be made before implementing features.

---

## 3. Separation of Concerns

Lunar Core focuses on data and functionality.

Presentation, layout, and styling are the responsibility of the theme, ensuring that content remains portable and easy to maintain.

---

## 4. Content First

Every technical decision should support content management and the reading experience.

Technology is a tool for presenting information more effectively, not an end in itself.

---

## 5. Quality Over Quantity

It is better to have a small number of well-designed features than a large number of features that are difficult to maintain.

---

## 6. Consistency

Choose solutions that are consistent, easy to understand, and easy to maintain over solutions that introduce unnecessary complexity.

---

## 7. Every Feature Has a Cost

Every feature affects maintenance, testing, documentation, compatibility, and performance.

For this reason, new features should provide value that justifies their long-term maintenance cost.

---

## 8. Performance by Default

Performance is considered from the very beginning of the design process.

CSS, JavaScript, and other assets should only be loaded when they are genuinely required.

---

## 9. WordPress Native

Lunar Core follows official WordPress standards and APIs whenever possible.

This includes Gutenberg, the Block API, and the WordPress Coding Standards.

---

## 10. Accessibility

Accessibility is considered from the beginning to ensure that every component can be used by as many people as possible.

---

## 11. Semantic HTML

Use clean, semantic HTML to support accessibility, SEO, and long-term maintainability.

---

## 12. Documentation Matters

Documentation is an essential part of the development process.

Important decisions and architectural changes should be documented clearly.

---

## 13. Keep It Simple

When multiple solutions are equally effective, choose the simplest one.

---

## 14. Long-Term Thinking

Every decision should consider the long-term sustainability of the project, including future growth in content, features, and users.
